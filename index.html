Proposed Audit Compliance Framework

Presented by Internal Audit

To: S.P. Madrid Corporation

Date: March 28, 2025

Introduction

Purpose: Improve team accountability and promote audit excellence

Proposal Includes:

  • Team-based non-compliance fines
  • Team-based recognition and incentives
  • Fine proceeds will go to the company

Goal: Build a culture of collective responsibility and excellence

Fine Structure – Team-Based Deductions

Initial Findings (1st Offense):

  • Requirement: Acknowledgment + action plan within 30 days
  • Penalty: PHP 10/day per unresolved finding (capped at PHP 500/finding)
  • Funds: Collected by the company

Objective: Promote shared responsibility and timely resolution

Fine Structure – Recurring Offenses

Second Audit (Same Findings):

  • Penalty: PHP 1,000 per unresolved finding

Objective: Escalate seriousness of repeated issues

Fine Structure – Continuous Non-Compliance

Subsequent Audits:

  • Penalty Increases: Additional PHP 1,000 per finding per audit
  • e.g., PHP 2,000 for 3rd audit, PHP 3,000 for 4th

Objective: Deter ongoing inaction with escalating penalties

Recognition Program – Team-Based Rewards

Audit Excellence Recognition (No Findings):

  • Rewards apply to the entire team
  • Certificate of Audit Excellence
  • Penalty Waiver Grace Period
  • Optional Public Recognition

Reward Details

  • Certificate of Audit Excellence: Formal recognition of the team’s flawless audit result
  • Penalty Waiver Grace Period: 15-day waiver for minor findings in the next audit (one-time use)
  • Public Recognition: Team acknowledged in internal/external reports (with consent)

Overall Summary – Proposed Audit Compliance Framework

The Internal Audit team proposes the implementation of a team-based Audit Compliance Framework aimed at improving accountability, ensuring timely resolution of audit findings, and recognizing outstanding performance.

This framework introduces a two-part system:

  • Non-Compliance Fine Policy: Applicable to teams with unresolved audit findings.
    • Fines begin at PHP 10/day per finding (capped at PHP 500) after the committed resolution deadline.
    • Recurring and unresolved findings escalate to PHP 1,000 in the second audit, increasing by PHP 1,000 per finding in every subsequent audit.
    • All penalties will be collected by the company, not individual deductions.
    • The focus is on team accountability, not individual blame.
  • Recognition Program for Audit Excellence: Incentives for teams with no findings.
    • Issuance of a Certificate of Audit Excellence.
    • 15-day penalty waiver for minor findings in the next audit.
    • Optional public recognition of the team’s compliance achievement.

Goal: To establish a culture of collaborative compliance, where teams are both accountable for unresolved issues and rewarded for audit excellence.
